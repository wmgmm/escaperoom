FACILITATOR BRIEFING — AI Escape Room
Issued by: Christopher Gravitas, IT Services
Classification: FACILITATOR EYES ONLY

======================================================================

WHAT THIS IS

A 30-minute competitive exercise. Participants use Microsoft Copilot's
image analysis to investigate six evidence artifacts, identify a
governance failure, and locate a missing grant application before a
countdown expires.

The scenario is fictional. The AI skills being practised are not.

======================================================================

SETUP

1. Direct participants to the website URL.
2. They register with their Cardiff University email and name.
3. The 30-minute countdown starts the moment they click through.
4. They work individually (or in pairs if you prefer — your call).

Participants can either download the artifact images directly from the
site (click any card) or photograph their screen and upload to Copilot.
Either works. Downloading is cleaner.

WHAT PARTICIPANTS SUBMIT

Four fields on the website:

I.   The Failure   — dropdown, one correct answer
II.  The Threat    — free text: what is at stake
III. The Location  — free text: where is the backup
IV.  Best Prompt   — the Copilot prompt that gave them the most
                     useful result (this is the actual learning
                     outcome you want to discuss)

======================================================================

THE CORRECT ANSWERS

I — The Failure: Unsanctioned AI Tool Usage

Albright purchased an AI license (£299, Ref RES-0847) without
authorisation. This triggered a departmental account freeze, which
blocked the grant submission.

II — The Threat: The £5M UKRI grant (GR-2025-0047) will lapse at
17:00 if the application is not filed. The Vice-Chancellor will be
extremely unhappy. That is putting it diplomatically.

III — The Location: Cabinet 3, Server Room B, North Wing

Confirmed by both the ledger (Exhibit D) and the floor plan fragments
(Exhibit E). The USB from the hotel meeting (Exhibit B) may also
contain a copy — Room 312, The Royal Conference Hotel, 18 November.

IV — Best Prompt: No single correct answer. This is the discussion
point. Look for prompts that are specific and instructive, e.g.:

  "Extract all handwritten text from this image and list it as
   bullet points"

  "Identify any names, dates, and monetary amounts visible in
   this document"

Vague prompts ("what does this show?") versus targeted ones is the
lesson.

======================================================================

JUDGING

The site records name, time taken, time remaining, and all four
answers. Fastest correct submission wins. Partially correct
submissions lose. The leaderboard is visible to all participants
after submission.

WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED (FULL STORY)

Dr Albright, under pressure to deliver the £5M UKRI grant proposal
(GR-2025-0047), purchased an AI writing tool without going through
procurement. The charge (£299, flagged as *UNAUTH* on the
departmental expenses, Ref RES-0847) triggered an automatic account
freeze. Prof. Whitmore (Research Governance) issued a formal
rejection on 14 November — the letter Albright crumpled and
presumably hoped no one would find.

A physical backup of the grant application exists: Cabinet 3,
Server Room B, North Wing. This was noted in the Authorised
Expenditure Register (page 47) and confirmed on the floor plan
fragments. There is also a USB drive from a meeting at The Royal
Conference Hotel, Room 312, 18 November — referenced on the coaster.

The grant can still be submitted if someone retrieves the backup
before 17:00. That someone is your participants.
