## YOUR OBJECTIVE

A governance crisis has occurred. A grant is missing. The Vice-Chancellor
is unhappy. You have 30 minutes and six pieces of evidence.

Use Microsoft Copilot to analyse each artifact. Identify the root cause,
what is at stake, and where the physical backup is hidden. Submit your
findings before the countdown expires.

That is all the briefing you require. The rest is on screen.

## ACCESSING MICROSOFT COPILOT

Open one of the following in your browser:

- **copilot.microsoft.com** — sign in with your Cardiff University account
- **Microsoft 365** — Copilot icon in the top right of any app
- **Windows 11** — Copilot button in the taskbar

You need to be signed in with your `@cardiff.ac.uk` account to access
image analysis. If it asks you to upgrade, you are in the wrong place.
The university licence covers this.

## HOW TO ANALYSE AN IMAGE

**Step 1 — Download the artifact**
Click any evidence card on the site. The file saves to your Downloads folder.

**Step 2 — Open Copilot and attach the image**
In the Copilot chat box, click the **paperclip / attachment icon**.
Select the file you just downloaded.

**Step 3 — Type your prompt and send**
Do not just ask "what is this?" That is not a prompt. That is a question
a confused person asks at an art gallery.

**Step 4 — Repeat for all six artifacts**
You may investigate in any order. Cross-reference what you find.

---

## WRITING A USEFUL PROMPT

The difference between a good prompt and a bad one is specificity.
Copilot will give you whatever you ask for. If you ask for nothing
in particular, it will give you nothing in particular.

**Instead of:**
> "What does this image show?"

**Try:**
> "Analyse this image. Extract every name, date, and monetary amount
> visible. List them as bullet points."

**Instead of:**
> "Is there anything important here?"

**Try:**
> "Read all handwritten text in this image exactly as written.
> Then identify which item appears to be flagged or highlighted
> and explain why it might be significant."

The prompt on the site is a starting point, not a ceiling.
The leaderboard shows everyone's best prompt after submission.
You will learn more from that column than from anything else today.

## WHAT TO SUBMIT

Four fields. One attempt. Choose carefully.

| Field | What it is |
|-------|-----------|
| The Failure | What caused the governance crisis — select from the dropdown |
| The Threat | What is at stake if this reaches the press |
| The Location | Where the physical backup of the grant is stored |
| Your Best Prompt | The exact Copilot prompt that gave you the most useful result |

The last field is the most important one for today's session.
It is also the one most people rush. Do not rush it.
